The Complexities of the Mind

Author: Lola-Rose Gooch

Read Time: 5 mins

The white figure stared into the buzzing skies, watching as the birds tried to move (but with little to no success). One by one, their black figures would fall. Slowly, but it would always result in the violent thud of the bird's limp body hitting the floor.

The yellow and salmon-coloured flowers around the figure watched with it. They pitied the birds, created to fly but destined to fall. But they also willed the skies to let them float, even if it lead to their immortal roots dying.

"Why would you do something if it ultimately lead to your death?" - The white figure

The flowers let silence come between them and the figure while they pondered.

"It is because they have a dream. Each and every one of them wishes to be able to fly, so that is what they will all try to do." - The flowers

The white figure looked up into the sky. He noticed a small little bird about to take flight. Before the figure could do anything to stop it, the bird began flying. Anticipating the heart-breaking thud, the figure closed its eyes. But the thud never came. The white figure looked up to the sky once more, to see one small but confidently-flying bird in the sky.
